# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Salespeople and account managers at a small-to-medium window treatment business (blinds, curtains, shutters, drapes). Two primary contexts: salespeople on mobile at customer homes filling in measure sheets and checking jobs; account managers on desktop reviewing the full pipeline, creating quotes, managing installers, and tracking revenue performance. Users are not technical — the tool needs to be fast and obvious.

## Product Purpose

Lusso replaces paper-based and spreadsheet job tracking with a full-workflow CRM: customer contacts → jobs → site measurements → quotes → client approval → product ordering → installer scheduling → installation → completion. Success means a salesperson can complete a measure sheet on-site in under two minutes, and an account manager can see the full pipeline state at a glance without training.

## Brand Personality

Premium trade tool. Precise, calm, and professional — the way a well-run operation feels from the inside. Not flashy. The name "Lusso" signals quality; the UI should earn that name through clarity and finish, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards with rainbow metric cards and gradient hero numbers
- Consumer app aesthetics (rounded corners everywhere, playful illustrations, bounce animations)
- Dark-pattern heavy CRMs that bury information behind tabs and modals
- AI-generated UIs with gratuitous gradient backgrounds on every surface

## Design Principles

1. **Field-first**: Every interaction must work one-handed on a phone at a client's home. Mobile is not a degraded fallback — it is the primary context for data entry.
2. **Pipeline clarity**: The most important question is always "where does this job stand?" Status should be visible at a glance without opening a record.
3. **Earn the premium name**: Lusso means luxury. Polish shows in typography, spacing rhythm, and the absence of visual clutter — not in decorative effects.
4. **Fast to complete, easy to review**: Salespeople complete tasks quickly; managers review thoroughly. Both needs are valid. Forms optimize for speed; reports optimize for overview.
5. **Trust through consistency**: Every action has a visible outcome. Saves confirm, deletes ask, errors explain. No ambiguity about whether something worked.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Keyboard navigation required for desktop account manager workflows. High contrast is a natural requirement for the dark teal sidebar. Reduce motion support for animated transitions.
